HOW TO WRITE A CURRICULUM VITAE

Curriculum vitae is a latin phrase that means "Course of Life"
And this document is a detailed summary of your professional journery

Common Structure

  • Personal information
  • Career objectives
  • Education
  • Skills
  • Projects
  • Achievements
  • Referees

Quick Tips

  • Keep it 1-2 pages
  • Use bullet points for readablity
  • Avoid grammar or spelling errors
  • Always customize your CV for the job you are applying for

HOW TO WRITE A COVER LETTER

This a document usually one page that you write and send along with your CV when applying for a job, internship, or scholarship

A cover explains why you are interested in the position, why you are a good fit, and encourages the employer to read your CV.

Common Structure

  • Your Contact Information
  • The Employer's Contact Information
  • Greeting
  • Opening Paragraph - Introduction
  • Body paragraph
  • Closing Paragraph >
  • Formal Closing

Example

Dear Hiring Manager ,
I am writing to apply for the Software Intern position at ABC tech.I am currently pursuing a Bachelor's degree in Software Engineering at Cavndish University Uganda.
During my academic journey, I have developed skills in web development, problem solving, and teamwork. I recently built a digital communication guide for students as part of a University project, which improved my HTML, CSS, and user experience skills.
I am excited about the opportunity to contribute to your team while continuing to learn.
Thank you for considering my application. I look forward to discussing how I can help at ABC tech.
Sincerely, Kunihira Albert.

Quick Tips

  • Tailor it to the job
  • Keep it short and focused
  • Highlight Achievements, Not just duties
  • Use professional language
  • Avoid repeating your CV

INTERVIEW TIPS

Before the interview

  1. Research the company

    • Learn about their mission, service, culture, and recent news
    • Know what the company values and tailor your answers accordingly
    • Learn the dress code accordingly

  2. Understand the Job Role

    • Read the job description carefully.
    • Prepare examples that show how your skills match the role

  3. Practice Common Questions

    • Tell me about yourself?
    • Why do you want this position?
    • What are your strengths and weaknesses?
    • Describe a time you solved a problem?

  4. Use the STAR method - For behavioral questions

    • Situation - What was happening?
    • Task - What was the goal?
    • Action - What did you do ?
    • Result - What happened?

  5. Dress appropriately

    • Wear clean, professional clothes (even the interview is online)
    • Avoid flashy accessories or overly casual outfits.

  6. Prepare questions to ask them

    • What does success look like in this role?
    • What are the next steps after the interview?

During the interview

  1. Be on Time

    • Arrive 10 - 15 minutes early or join the video call early

  2. Be Confident, Not Arrogant

    • Maintain eye contact, smile, and sit up straight.
    • Speak clearly and calmly

  3. Listen Carefully

    • Don't interrupt.
    • Pause before you answer to collect your thoughts.

  4. Show Enthusiasm

    • Employers love to hire motivated and passsionate people.

Bonus Tips

  • Bring a copy of your CV and any relevant documents
  • Be honest - if you don't know something, say so
  • Turn off your phone before the interview starts
  • Practice with a friend or in front of a mirror

After the Interview

  1. Send a Thank-You Email

    • Within 24 hours, send a short thank-you note to appreciate the opportunity

      Dear [Interview's Name], Thank you for taking the time to speak with me today. I truly enjoy learning more about the [Positions] role at [Company Name]. I'm excited about the possibility of joining your team and contributing to your goals.

INTERNSHIP PREPARATION

INTERNSHIP PREPARATION GUIDE FOR STUDENTS

  1. Understand Why Interships Matter

    • Build real-world experience
    • Apply your classroom knowledge
    • Grow your professional network
    • Discover what kind of work you enjoy
    • Boost your chances of future employment

  2. Prepare Your Application Material

    • CV.
    • Cover letter
    • Portfolio(e.g, for designers, developers, writers)
    • Academic Transcript or Certificates

  3. Start searching Early

    • School or University or career centres
    • Job websites like BrighterMonday, LinkedIn, Glassdoor
    • Company Websites
    • Referrals from lecturers or friends

  4. Practice Interview Skills

    • Rehearse common interview questions
    • Use the STAR method to structure your answers
    • Practice mock interviews with friends or mentors
    • Result - What happened?

  5. Prepare for the Work Environment

    • Learn basic professional etiquette(communication, dress code, timekeeping)
    • Understand teamwork and reporting.
    • Learn the tools and technologies commonly used in your field

  6. Set Personal Goals

    Before your interships starts, ask yourself

    • What do I want to learn?
    • What skills do I want to improve
    • What kind of people do I want to meet?

  7. Be Professional From Day One

    • Arrive early and stay organized
    • Ask questions and show willingness to learn
    • Take notes during meetings or tasks
    • Communicate clearly with your supervisor

  8. Keep a Learning Journal

    Track what you learn each day or week:

    • New skills or tools used
    • Challenges faced and how you solved them
    • Team experiences or mentorship received

  9. Request a Recomendation Letter

    At the end of your internship, ask your supervisor or manager for letter or for a letter of recommendation to help with future job or scholarships applications.

  10. Reflect and Grow

    After your internship

    • Review your goals - Did you achieve them?
    • Update your CV with your new experience
    • Stay in touch with people you worked with
